Maps

Researchers often want to see temporal or spatial patterns of publication. Blue Mountain Maps will let them plot dates and places of publication on interactive maps and timelines.

Our bibliographic records already encode dates of publication in a standard format (w3cdtf) and places of publication as they appear in extant catalog records. The prototype of Blue Mountain Maps will use this already-existing data and off-the-shelf software to allow users to plot and compare publication timelines at various levels of granularity (author level, issue level, and title level) and to plot places of publication on a spatial map, using the Google Maps API.

The final version of Blue Mountain Maps will integrate Blue Mountain's data with date information gleaned from linked-data sources to give Blue Mountain's resources more context. It will also use the results of automated named-entity recognition to generate “aboutness maps” that can provide geospatial context for articles and other forms of content in Blue Mountain's magazines.
