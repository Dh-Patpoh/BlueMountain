Radar

Word clouds are a popular way of displaying the relative frequency of string patterns in a corpus. We say string patterns and not words, because the concept of word is a complex one. Nevertheless, word clouds are often used, for example, as a way of quickly judging the relevance of a text to a particular inquiry. They can be a useful tool for quickly assessing the “aboutness” of a text , or for comparing the distribution of strings in different texts.

The Blue Mountain Radar application will extract full-text data from Blue Mountain’s XML database to produce word clouds at various elevations: at the collection level, the title level, the issue level, and the constituent level. The application will allow users to select multiple objects to compare (different magazines, different issues, or different articles, for example).
