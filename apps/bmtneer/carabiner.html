Carabiner

Tracing connections among people, be they speakers in a play or contributors to a magazine, is a popular application of digital technology to humanities research. Blue Mountain’s bibliographic metadata is unusually detailed: we have cataloged every issue to the item level, which means we have recorded the title and creator of every article, poem, and illustration. The Blue Mountain Carabiner tool will use that detailed metadata to let users explore the complex webs of relations among titles, authors, artists, languages, and nationalities. For example, magazines often centered around coteries of writers or artists whose composition often changed over the life of the magazine. Being able to see how contributors clustered in a magazine can make it possible to track the changing character, purpose, and politics of a magazine. It is also useful to see the trajectory of an individual’s contributions: when a writer was most productive, for example, or when a writer shifted allegiance from one magazine or group to another.
